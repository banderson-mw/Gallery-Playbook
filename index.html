What This Is

The Masterworks gallery channel is a physical sales model built around invite-only evening events. Wealthy prospects attend gallery openings at curated locations, view fine art held by Masterworks, and meet with financial advisors trained to convert interest into investment.

This operating system is the authoritative reference for anyone working in or with the gallery channel — from the first day of hiring through daily event execution and long-term expansion.

Hiring
The profile, the process, and who moves it forward.

The Right Profile

The gallery FA role is not a traditional wealth management position. The model demands a short-cycle, high-activity closer — someone who can work a room, qualify fast, follow up hard, and log everything. A relationship-builder who waits for trust to develop before asking for business is the wrong fit.

Must-Haves

  • Demonstrated high call/meeting volume in a previous role
  • Comfort closing in a compressed timeline (same week or next week)
  • Genuine interest in art or luxury — not required to be an expert, but must be credible on the floor
  • CRM discipline — logs without being reminded
  • Series 65 or ability to obtain quickly (discuss with Scott on licensing requirements)

Red Flags

  • Previous role was primarily inbound or account management
  • Defines success as "building a book over time"
  • Resistant to activity tracking or structured accountability
  • No evidence of working a high-volume pipeline

Scott's Lane

Specific comp packages, offer letters, and base salary decisions route to Scott. This section documents the framework and bridge metric used to model variable comp.

The Meeting Value Framework

The model uses a bridge metric — revenue per meeting — to connect activity to economic output. This gives the comp model a grounded basis that isn't purely subjective.

$1.576M
Total revenue baseline (modeling period)
134
Logged meetings in period
$11,764
Modeled revenue per meeting

At 25–30 meetings per week per FA, the implied revenue capacity per FA is significant. Variable comp structures should be built against meeting volume and quality rating thresholds — not just raw revenue, which lags too far behind activity to be a useful real-time signal.

Variable Comp Design Principles

  • Activity metrics (meetings, quality rating) should precede revenue metrics in the payout structure — they're the leading indicator
  • Comp should reward quality over quantity — a 4/5-rated meeting tier should pay more per deal than a 2/3-rated meeting tier
  • New FA ramp comp should be front-loaded (higher base, lower variable) and shift toward performance-weighted over 6 months
  • All comp structures finalize with Scott

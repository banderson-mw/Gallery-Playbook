What This Is

The Masterworks gallery channel is a physical sales model built around invite-only evening events held three nights per week, 4–7pm. HNW prospects with investable portfolios of $3M or more attend these events, view works from the Masterworks portfolio, and meet with financial advisors trained to convert interest into investment.

This operating system is the authoritative reference for everyone in the gallery channel — from the first day of onboarding through daily execution and long-term expansion.

Prospects are sourced through outbound cold email to HNW individuals and through co-branded events with businesses and centers of influence — wealth managers, family offices, PE/VC firms, luxury service providers, and professional networks whose clients match the target profile. The Partner Events program details eligible categories.

The Team

Each gallery location runs with four roles. Every person on the floor has a defined lane — the model only works when each role is operating within it.

Sr. Financial Advisor Owns rank 4–5 prospect relationships — the highest-value attendees. Responsible for converting gallery attendance into investment through direct relationship development. Call Strategy & Scripting
What to say, when to say it, and what you're trying to learn.
The Standard

Every call has one job. Know what it is before you dial. The goal of a post-event call is never to "check in" — it's to qualify, advance, or make a decision.

Post-Event: Prospect Booked a Meeting at the Gallery

This is the first formal touch. The prospect is warm — they showed up, they met you, they agreed to a conversation. The job of this call is to qualify suitability, get a sense of portfolio size, and prime them for the allocation conversation that follows. By the end of this call you should know: are they interested, is this suitable, what are the next steps to advance them toward investment?

Call Goals

  • Confirm their interest is genuine (vs. polite gallery curiosity)
  • Establish suitability — investable assets, investment experience, timeline
  • Gauge portfolio size — enough to have a meaningful allocation conversation
  • Determine path: advance to recommendation call, warm up more, or DQ to self-serve
  • Set clear next step before hanging up

Outcomes After This Call

SignalStage MoveNext Step
Interested, suitable, portfolio confirmed→ Active RelationshipBook recommendation call within the week
Interested but needs more warmingStay in Active RelationshipSet specific next touchpoint — event invite, follow-up article, check-in date
Not suitable / below threshold→ Self ServeWarm handoff — acknowledge they're not the right fit for the current model, point to self-serve platform
ScriptPost-Event Booked — Qualification Call (~15 min)
Open
"[Name], great to connect — really glad you could make it [Tuesday/Thursday]. I wanted to get a few minutes with you before our meeting to make sure we use your time well when we sit down."
Confirm Interest
"Tell me what drew you in — was it a specific piece, the model, or just curious to see what we're doing?"
Listen for: genuine investment interest vs. social curiosity. Adjust depth of qualification accordingly.
Qualify Suitability
"Our investors typically have [X] in investable assets and are looking to diversify into alternatives — does that resonate with where you are right now?"
Needs detail: confirm minimum investable asset threshold and accreditation language with Scott/compliance.
Gauge Allocation Readiness
"When you think about your current portfolio — are you actively looking to put capital to work, or more in an exploratory phase right now?"
Not asking for a number. Asking for intent and timeline.
Set the Next Step
"Based on what you've told me, I think our meeting is going to be really productive — I'll come prepared with a specific allocation we can talk through. Does [day/time] still work for you?"

Post-Event: Prospect Did Not Book a Meeting

This is a 3-minute call. One goal: get the 15-minute warm-up call on the calendar. Don't qualify here. Don't pitch here. The meeting is the product — sell that.

Call Goals

  • Reconnect briefly on the gallery experience
  • Make the ask for a 15-minute call — frame it as low-stakes
  • Get a specific time on the calendar before hanging up
ScriptPost-Event No-Book — 3-Minute Scheduling Call
Open
"[Name], this is [FA] from Masterworks — we met at the gallery [Tuesday/Thursday]. Do you have 2 minutes?"
Reference the Event
"Glad you could make it — I wanted to follow up while it was fresh. Did anything from the evening stand out to you?"
Let them talk. One sentence response. Don't go deep here.
Make the Ask
"I'd love to get 15 minutes with you — not a pitch, just a quick conversation to see if what we're doing is relevant to where you are. Are you free [specific day] or [specific day] this week?"
Give two options. Do not ask "when are you free" — it pushes the decision to them.
If They Push Back
"Totally fair — it's 15 minutes and if it's not a fit, you'll know quickly. What does next week look like?"

Weekly Check-In Structure

The weekly check-in is a working session, not a status report. It should take 20–30 minutes and follow this structure:

5m
Numbers First
No commentary yet — just the data

FA walks through the six tracking items for the week. Sr. FA listens. No coaching until the picture is complete.

10m
Pipeline Walk
Deal by deal on anything stuck or unclear

What's the next step on each active deal? Is it realistic? Has it moved? If not, why not — and what changes this week?

10m
One Thing to Fix
Coach on the highest-leverage gap

Don't try to fix everything. Pick the single behavior or gap most affecting output this week. Make it specific, make it actionable, confirm understanding before ending the call.

5m
Goals for Next Week
FA states them, Sr. FA confirms

Specific, numeric. Not "I'm going to work on follow-ups." "I'll send follow-up on all 12 active deals by Tuesday and log them same-day."

Hiring
The profile, the process, and the career path.

The Right Profile

The gallery FA role is not a traditional wealth management position. The model demands a short-cycle, high-activity closer — someone who can work a room, qualify fast, follow up hard, and log everything. A relationship-builder who waits for trust to develop before asking for business is the wrong fit.

Must-Haves

  • Demonstrated high call/meeting volume in a previous role — can cite a number
  • Comfort closing in a compressed timeline (same week or next week)
  • Genuine comfort in a gallery/art environment — not an expert, but credible on the floor
  • CRM discipline — logs without being reminded
  • Series 65 or ability to obtain quickly

Red Flags

  • Previous role was primarily inbound or account management
  • Defines success as "building a book over time"
  • Resistant to activity tracking or structured accountability
  • Can't cite their weekly meeting or call volume from their last role
